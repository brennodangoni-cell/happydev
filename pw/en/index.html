What's inside

Six chapters for organised, effective work.

TaskFlow keeps things simple. Each chapter introduces one clear system or method, explains why it works and shows you how to apply it immediately — so your task management improves from the very first day you read the guide.

Chapter 01
🧠

Why Most Task Systems Fail

The common reasons people struggle with task management — overloaded to-do lists, no clear priorities, starting the day reactively. How simple changes to your approach make a meaningful difference to how much you actually get done each day.

Chapter 02
📋

Capturing and Organising Tasks

How to build a reliable system for capturing every task so nothing falls through the cracks — inbox organisation, categorising by project and context, using digital and analogue tools effectively, and keeping your task list manageable rather than overwhelming.

Chapter 03
🎯

Prioritisation Methods That Work

How to decide what to work on first — the MIT (Most Important Task) method, the Eisenhower Matrix for urgent vs. important decisions, how to identify your highest-impact tasks each day and avoid spending time on low-value work that feels productive but isn't.

Chapter 04
⏱️

Planning Your Day Effectively

A simple daily planning routine — how to start each day with clarity, time-block your most important tasks, build in realistic buffer time, handle unexpected tasks without losing your plan, and close each day with a brief review that sets up tomorrow before you finish today.

Chapter 05
📅

Weekly Planning and Review

The weekly review practice that keeps your whole system running — how to process the week's backlog, carry forward unfinished tasks, set weekly goals, plan the coming week before it arrives, and maintain momentum across multiple projects without losing track of any of them.

Chapter 06
🌱

Building Lasting Task Habits

How to make your task system a consistent habit rather than something you only use when things are going well — habit stacking, minimum effective dose routines, maintaining your system during busy periods, and adapting your approach as your work and life circumstances change.

Yes — Chapter 2 on organising tasks covers managing multiple projects specifically, including how to keep different workstreams visible without overwhelming a single list. Chapter 5 on weekly review is particularly useful for anyone juggling several ongoing commitments simultaneously.
